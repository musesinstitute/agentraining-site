Common Questions

Before you ask, we'll answer

Including the question every skeptical agent asks first.

Why should I use your platform instead of just opening ChatGPT or Claude myself and practicing?
You can absolutely improvise a roleplay with any general AI chatbot — but improvising isn't the same as training. A generic chatbot doesn't know what "good" looks like in an insurance or real estate sales conversation, won't push back the way a real skeptical client does, and at the end it will just tell you "great job" without telling you why or what to fix. AgentTraining AI scores every response against a consistent framework built on Sun Tzu's strategic thinking and Dale Carnegie's principles of influence — the same standard every time, so your progress is actually measurable instead of just a feeling.
I've been selling for 10 years. Isn't this just for new agents?
No. The scoring framework doesn't get easier or simpler based on experience — it's the same standard for everyone. Veteran agents often discover their blind spots are in places they stopped paying attention to years ago: jumping to persuasion before fully understanding an objection, or pushing toward a close instead of letting the client arrive there themselves. Practicing against a consistent standard is valuable precisely because experience can create bad habits that nobody is correcting anymore.
How is the AI client different from a real client?
It isn't trying to be identical — it's trying to be useful. The AI is built with industry-specific knowledge (life insurance, annuities, real estate negotiation) and responds the way real clients actually push back, not with generic objections. You can fail safely here, as many times as you need to, before that same conversation happens with a real client and real money on the line.
What if I don't like the feedback I get?
Then it's doing its job. The point of structured feedback isn't to make you feel good — it's to show you specifically where the conversation went off track, so you can fix it before it costs you a real deal. Every session includes both what you did well and what to improve.
